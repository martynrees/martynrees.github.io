    9800 WLC Cat9k AP Default Console Baud Rate

    The current long term release of the IOS-XE 9800 WLC is feature packed, but tucked away in the release notes is a one liner that is sure to catch people out in the near future(or me the other day): From this release, the default console baud rate of the 802.11AX APs is changed from 9600 bps to 115200 bps. What does this mean? Well, digging a bit deeper, it only covers the Cat9k range of AP’s, so 9120, 9130,9136, 916x, etc AND It only impacts new (or factory reset) AP’s when they join the controller running 17.12. So if AP’s are already joined to the controller and are upgraded from 17.9.x to 17.12.x then they will still use the old 9600bps rate, but then if you add a new AP to the controller, that will use the new setting of 115200bps. If an upgraded AP is set back to factory default though and joins the controller again, it will assume the new 115200bps config. So you could/will end up with some AP’s on the old 9600bps config and some on the new 115200bps config. Or, once upgraded to 17.12 or higher then factory reset all AP’s from the GUI, and then you’ll have a standard across the board, no matter which AP you connect to (that’s a Cat9k AP anyway, Wave1/2 are not impacted by this!).     CWISA

    This will be a short post, summarising my experience with the CWISA exam, which I passed recently. The Certified Wireless IoT Solutions Administrator, or CWISA is the assosciate level exam that starts you on the CWNP IoT certification track. It is also a requirement for CWNE certification. As it’s an assosciate level exam, the depth isn’t huge, but the topics covered are broad. I took the newly released CWISA-102 exam. This exam will stay current until September 2025, as it was only recently refreshed. For study, it’s first important to thoroughly read through the exam objectives to understand the topics covered, and the depth of knowledge required. As I’ve already had quite a bit of experience with a lot of the topics through my home automation journey, this wasn’t a hard exam to study for, and using the study guide from the previous version of the exam was sufficient for me, with a couple of read throughs. My prior knowledge of zigbee, as well as having previously passed CWNA certainly helped to speed up the process. I always ensure that I get the official practice exams, as they are a great gauge of where i’m at in my preperation, and they also help to prepare you for the typical language used in the exam. This is always important for me, as it gets my head in the right space and can help for some of those questions that are worded a little funny that you see in ever vendors exam. Even with my confidence, I was still nervous heading in to the exam, as you do wonder if you are being over confident in your prior knowledge, and will it actually stack up when the time counts. Thankfully it was more than enough to get me through, and I was soon able to breath a sigh of relief when the confirmation that I had passed came up on the screen with a 51/60.
