    Meraki Intelligent Capture

    September 14, 2024 · 2 min · 399 words · Martyn Rees | Suggest Changes

    Packet captures have existed for a long time on the Meraki platform, and have saved me great amounts of travel time over the years. But now they have had a refresh and not just a GUI makeover (ok well it did get that as well.), but new features added too. Proactive PCAP and cloud storage are the main stars here.
    The old view served us well, and was clean, easy to use and just did its job. Now called Intelligent Capture, it still does what the old feature did, but with a newer fresher look, and now includes cloud storage (so no more losing files) and the newest feature, Proactive PCAP’s that we will have a look at in the next post. Nothing overly exciting in the Manual capture, but if we select to save to the cloud then it will be retained for 90 days before being deleted.

    As at this stage the Intelligent Capture feature is still in early access, so you have to enable it through Organization > Configure > Early Access. You’ll also need to make sure that you are running at least MR30 or higher, which is going to be a non issue for most these days anyway.
    You can then browse to Network-wide > Monitor > Intelligent Capture (best to do a page refresh after enabling so that the side bar reloads) Whilst you do still have the download option as you’ve always done I still prefer to save to cloud and then be able to download from the stored captures page. In testing I just found that this performed better and I didn’t have the old issues of browsers not coping with the streaming file as the pcap was taken, ending up with a corrupt capture that I had to re-take.
    One thing to remember is that you are capturing from the AP, so if the AP can’t hear it then it can’t capture it. Whilst it’s great that you can capture from multiple AP’s at a time and stitch it all together in one PCAP, it’s also important to remember that you still might not capture everything or what you are looking for, so this doesn’t necessarily replace capturing from your laptop next to the problem device. For that I’d reccomend using Airtool as it’s the simplest and fastest way to take captures on a mac or through a WLAN Pi.
